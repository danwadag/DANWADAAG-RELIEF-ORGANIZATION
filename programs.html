Core Programmatic Operations

Technical frameworks and multi-sector strategies across South-West State.

Sector 01

Primary Health Care & Nutrition

Strategic Focus: DRO scales up clinical accessibility to curb acute child and mother mortality rates stemming from preventable disease clusters. Our framework acts as an operational defense against endemic disease surges.

Operational Targets:

  • Deploying targeted mobile outreach clinics into isolated IDP camp grids and rural host areas.
  • Comprehensive management, diagnostic screening, and specialized supplementary therapeutic feeding for children under five, pregnant women, and lactating mothers.
  • Systematic delivery of localized maternal healthcare instruction, micronutrient therapy, and community breastfeeding training.
Sector 02

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)

Strategic Focus: For communities displaced by ongoing conflict or severe drought cyclical patterns, safe water infrastructure is the ultimate foundation for survival. DRO builds community-managed sanitation systems to systematically reduce diarrheal disease loads.

Key Framework Focus Areas:

  • Safe Water Supply: Delivering clean water for drinking, cooking, personal hygiene, and household usage.
  • Gender-Segregated Infrastructure: Building and maintaining safe, family-unit or gender-segregated latrines to protect women, girls, and children.
  • Disease Control & Solid Waste: Running active surveillance alongside vector-breeding control via site drainage and garbage management.
  • School-Based WASH: Restoring hygienic latrine grids inside schools to keep girls enrolled and active in learning.
Sector 03

Child Protection Frameworks

Strategic Focus: Establishing institutional networks and family-focused structures to safeguard children from systemic risk, labor exploitation, and conflict vulnerabilities. We treat child institutionalization as a strict measure of last resort.

Operational Benchmarks:

  • Designing localized tracking databases and custom information systems for community emergency protection monitoring.
  • Empowering local child protection committees to monitor child right vulnerabilities.
  • Active interventions against traditional harmful mechanisms, including child marriages and female genital mutilation (FGM).
Sector 04

Humanitarian Program Cycle (HPC)

In compliance with global standards, all emergency responses deployed by DRO follow a strict 5-stage lifecycle configuration:

  1. Rapid multi-sector needs assessment and field-level verification analysis.
  2. Strategic response planning.
  3. Resource mobilization and donor coordination tracking.
  4. Field-level deployment and rigorous project monitoring.
  5. Operational reviews, third-party evaluations, and data adjustments.

Immediate assistance items include clean water supplies, emergency medical packs, tents, food items, and critical hygiene materials.
