Sector 1 [span_32](start_span)

Primary Health Care & Nutrition[span_32](end_span)

[span_33](start_span)

Objective: Improve availability of and access to health care services for IDPs and host communities, focusing heavily on vulnerable groups like children through mobile clinics and the direct promotion of essential health services[span_33](end_span). [span_34](start_span)Our work targets common infections like malaria, measles, and diarrhea which precipitate acute malnutrition among children and infants[span_34](end_span).

Operational Actions:

    [span_35](start_span)
  • Management and prevention of malnutrition among children under five and pregnant/lactating mothers[span_35](end_span).
    • [span_36](start_span)
  • Delivery of proper nutrition education and training on the absolute importance of exclusive breastfeeding[span_36](end_span).
Sector 2 [span_37](start_span)[span_38](start_span)

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)[span_37](end_span)[span_38](end_span)

[span_39](start_span)

Objective: For people displaced by conflict, access to clean water and appropriate sanitation facilities are among the most urgent needs[span_39](end_span). [span_40](start_span)Without enough safe water, it is difficult to maintain health and ward off illnesses, while poor sanitation causes rapid disease spikes[span_40](end_span). [span_41](start_span)[span_42](start_span)DRO specializes in WASH to protect public health and support dignified living conditions[span_41](end_span)[span_42](end_span).

Key Targets:

    [span_43](start_span)
  • Supplying safe water for drinking, cooking, personal hygiene, and household cleaning[span_43](end_span).
    • [span_44](start_span)
  • Providing and maintaining safe, gender-segregated toilets or family units to protect women, girls, men, and boys at all times[span_44](end_span).
    • [span_45](start_span)
  • Active disease surveillance and solid waste management to eliminate vector-breeding grounds where disease-carrying mosquitoes or vermin multiply[span_45](end_span).
    • [span_46](start_span)
  • Ensuring safe WASH infrastructures in schools so girls are not discouraged from attending[span_46](end_span).

    [span_52](start_span)
  • Creating explicit tracking databases and management information systems (MIS) for active protection monitoring[span_52](end_span).
