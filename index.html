Section II: Health & Nutrition Primary Health Care (PHC) Manual

1. Primary Health Care Program Objectives

[span_51](start_span)

The overarching strategic directive of this desk is to improve the baseline availability of and direct access to high-quality healthcare service systems for internally displaced populations (IDPs) and host communities[span_51](end_span). [span_52](start_span)Focus metrics prioritize children under five and pregnant or lactating mothers (PLWs) through regular mobile field clinics and health promotions[span_52](end_span).

2. Direct Target Interventions

    [span_53](start_span)
  • Disease Vector Suppression: Direct medical management and prevention of prevalent regional infections including malaria, measles, and diarrhea, which break down children's physical systems and precipitate acute malnutrition[span_53](end_span).
    • [span_54](start_span)
  • Nutrition Education Field Delivery: Conducting structured community workshops to deliver proper nutrition science and teach the vital importance of exclusive breastfeeding during early developmental cycles[span_54](end_span).

Section III: Child Protection & Safeguarding Framework

1. Core Implementation Objectives

[span_55](start_span)

To institutionalize essential services and build strong local structures for outreach, institutional care systems, family-based options, counseling, and social support channels at national, regional, state, and district levels[span_55](end_span).

    [span_56](start_span)
  • Enhancing capacities of all ground functionaries, including public administrators, service providers, local government bodies, police, and the regional judiciary[span_56](end_span).
    • [span_57](start_span)
  • Forming secure database registries and Management Information Systems (MIS) with child tracking parameters for effective oversight[span_57](end_span).
    • [span_58](start_span)
  • Upholding privacy and confidentiality across all operational service delivery stages[span_58](end_span).
    • [span_59](start_span)
  • Enforcing absolute non-discrimination and non-stigmatization across religious, ethnic, cultural, and socio-economic lines[span_59](end_span).
  • Institutionalization Last Resort Rule: The manual explicitly guides that shifting focus away from institutional centers towards family or community care alternatives is central. [span_60](start_span)Institutional placement must be treated strictly as a measure of last resort[span_60](end_span).

Section IV: Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene (WASH) Engineering Manual

[span_61](start_span)

For populations displaced by sudden conflict, access to clean safe water lines and sanitation networks are immediate priorities[span_61](end_span). [span_62](start_span)Lacking these features deprives families of changing temporary camp shelters into dignified homes[span_62](end_span). [span_63](start_span)Safe water is essential for drinking, cooking, personal hygiene, and stopping rapid water-source contamination[span_63](end_span).

1. Five Pillars of WASH Operations

    [span_64](start_span)
  • Safe Water Supply: Delivering clean water for drinking, food preparation, and personal household cleaning metrics[span_64](end_span).
    • [span_65](start_span)
  • Gender-Segregated Infrastructure: Providing and maintaining latrines or toilets separated clearly by gender or familial units, designed to remain safe for women, girls, men, and boys at all hours[span_65](end_span).
    • [span_66](start_span)
  • Surveillance Protocols: Active tracking of disease spread and water-quality testing metrics during regional outbreaks[span_66](end_span).
    • [span_67](start_span)
  • Site Engineering Control: Enforcing solid waste management and camp site drainage to get rid of standing pools where mosquitoes or disease vermin breed[span_67](end_span).
    • [span_68](start_span)
  • Hygiene Promotion: Empowering displaced populations to lead neighborhood sanitation mobilization networks[span_68](end_span).

Section V: Reducing Rural Poverty (COM Framework Alignment)

[span_69](start_span)[span_70](start_span)

Aligning with established international strategies (including references like COM(2002)429 final), our strategy focuses on the reality that three-quarters of populations in extreme distress live in rural setups where hunger is prominent[span_69](end_span)[span_70](end_span). [span_71](start_span)Rural poverty is a multi-sided issue defined by low consumption margins, poor productivity, inequality in asset ownership, degradation of natural forests, and political exclusion[span_71](end_span).

1. Five Policy Pillars

    [span_72](start_span)
  1. Promoting broad-based rural economic growth vectors[span_72](end_span).
    2. [span_73](start_span)
  2. Ensuring equitable access to productive assets, micro-finance channels, markets, and land rights[span_73](end_span).
    3. [span_74](start_span)[span_75](start_span)
  3. Investing in human capital by renovating school spaces and rural medical clinics[span_74](end_span)[span_75](end_span).
    4. [span_76](start_span)
  4. Mainstreaming long-term environmental assessments directly into programming[span_76](end_span).
    5. [span_77](start_span)
  5. Addressing structural social exclusion by backing decentralized, highly participative institutions[span_77](end_span).
